# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective gym members researching a local Centurion-area gym, primarily working professional couples 26-35 (the questionnaire's "ideal member"), plus students, pensioners, and beginners. They're comparing options before committing, often on mobile, deciding whether to call, WhatsApp, or just show up for a free trial. The site's secondary surface, `/dashboard`, serves the gym owner reviewing site analytics — a distinct product-register tool, not a guest-facing experience, and should be treated with product-register rules when worked on directly.

## Product Purpose

A brochure / lead-generation site for an offline, family-run personal training gym (Bossie's Gym, Hennopspark, Centurion). No member portal, booking, or e-commerce — success is measured in enquiries and free-trial sign-ups via Join Online, Call, WhatsApp, and Start a Free Trial, not pageviews or session depth.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, Minimal, Energetic, Community-Focused (the client's own words, from the onboarding questionnaire). Confident without being corporate — "small on purpose," favoring real coaching and real community over chasing growth. Family warmth (Boshoff name, real address, real hours) paired with an athletic edge (red + steel-blue pulled directly from the real gym logo, near-black base).

## Anti-references

No fake testimonials, no stock-photo gloss, no invented stats or credentials — every claim on the site is traceable to the client's questionnaire or a confirmed follow-up; unconfirmed facts (trainer bios, photography) show explicit "TBD" placeholders rather than invented content. Avoid corporate big-box-gym blandness (mass-market sameness) and influencer-fitness hype / hard-sell landing pages.

## Design Principles

1. Visible honesty over fake polish — explicit placeholders beat invented content.
2. Small on purpose — confident minimalism, not corporate scale-signaling.
3. Family warmth with an athletic edge — red + steel-blue + near-black, energetic but not flashy.
4. Every fact traceable — grounded copy, zero invented claims.
5. Low-friction conversion over hard sell — WhatsApp / call / free-trial CTAs, never aggressive sales tactics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline. Focus-visible outlines on all interactive elements, accessible form field labels, and `prefers-reduced-motion` respected globally (already implemented per README — this formalizes it as the standing requirement going forward).
